How It Works

01 DEPLOY
Plant a Bait
"Generate a decoy file i.e DOCX, PDF, XLSX, QR code, or SQL_dump and place it in a location an attacker is likely to discover during reconnaissance or lateral movement."
02 MONITOR
Watch for Access
"Each bait carries a unique token. The moment it is opened, executed, or scanned, a callback fires to the SilentHook server."
03 ALERT
Instant Notification
"The trigger is logged with the attacker's IP, timestamp, user agent, and hostname. An alert email is dispatched to the owner immediately."
04 INVESTIGATE
Forensic Analysis
"Review the Alert Email & Analytics dashboards for a full picture of the event, who accessed it, when, from where, and how. Use this information to scope the breach and inform your response."

Project Vision

Why SilentHook?
Most security tools try to keep attackers out. SilentHook assumes they're already in. As a deception layer in a Defence in Depth strategy, SilentHook plants decoy files and tokens inside your environment operating behind your existing defences.
Unlike traditional detection tools, it generates near-zero false positives. No alerts unless something is touched. And if something is touched — it's not a maybe. It's a confirmed intrusion.
Academic Goal
Demonstrate practical application of deception technology concepts tokens, honeyfiles, and file integrity monitoring in a unified, production-grade platform built on Flask, MariaDB, and vanilla JS.
Security Goal
Provide lightweight decoy assets i.e files, tokens, and traps as a deception layer within a Defence in Depth strategy.
SilentHook supplements existing controls such as firewalls, IDS, and SIEM solutions, without requiring enterprise-level infrastructure or changes to the current security stack.
